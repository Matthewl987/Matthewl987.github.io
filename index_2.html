intro

The New York City Subway system is a crazy, little city. It is a transit system in New York City that we all do not want to take, yet we do because it is an integral transportation system through the 5 boroughs. There is something about New York’s subway system that distinguishes itself greatly from other transit systems around the world. It encapsulates the essence of New York City– the energy, diversity, and complexity. The vastly different interconnected train lines that are deeply rooted into many parts of the borough brings together all 5 borough’s cultures. The system is freely accessible in almost all parts of the city. Pay $2.90– or not and jump the turnstile– and you will be free to use the transit system however you like.
